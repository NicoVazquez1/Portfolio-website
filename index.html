  • Agrolo

    This project aims to streamline the process of recording fruit weight, revenue generated, worker payments, tray quantity, client information, and delivery type in a blueberry field. I myself worked in such a field a few years ago and witnessed firsthand how slow the process was and how it could be improved.

    So, the way it worked was as follows: every 2 hours, a truck would enter the field ready to be filled with the fruit. The workers would approach the truck one by one and submit their trays filled with fruit for weighing. The data would be recorded on paper, and at the end of the working day, all the calculations would be done to determine the payments for each worker, the total amount of fruit collected, and the generated revenue.

    Understanding this, the idea of creating an app came to mind. With the app, all the data could be recorded and automatically processed, eliminating the need for manual calculations.

    I proposed this idea to the field owner, and he liked it. Here is the design:


    I also created the entity-relationship, in this, one worker or 'trabajadores' can have multiples 'entregas', and 'entregas' can have only one 'despacho' or truck, etc...

    Then I created the users stories describing the problems to be solved and the budget estimating the amount of hours of work

    I based my architecture on MVC:

    The next step was creating the connection. For that, I used Entity Framework, and to be honest, I thought it would be more difficult. Then, I implemented security by creating the JWT and making the necessary configurations. Afterwards, I created the endpoints, connected to the database in the services folder, and whenever there's a need for a business operation, I handle it through the NegocioService class, which contains all the business logic.

    Finally, I conducted tests using Postman:

    Currently, the project is running smoothly on the server. For the interface, I'm going to train another person in the basics of programming to assist me in its development. Unfortunately, the project is on hold due to financial issues with the client. However, there is hope that the project will resume if the business model changes. There is a risk involved, but I like taking risks.

    Creating a 3D videogame in Unity

    I created this video game to put my programming and Unity skills into practice.

    Cannon is a 3D game where you shoot barrels that appear on the screen using your cannon. In the game mechanics, you can switch between three types of ammunition:

    Long-range Shot: a single bullet ideal for precision. (Image: path-to-image)

    Rapid Shot: multiple bullets with dispersion, ideal for quickly hitting multiple nearby targets. (Image: path-to-image)

    Air Explosion: explosive ammunition ideal for hitting multiple targets with a single blow. (Image: path-to-image)

    As time passes, the speed increases, and the barrels appear in different forms. To achieve this, I used the strategy pattern. (Code photo: path-to-image)

    There are also barrels that provide coins for earning more points, ammunition, or power-ups. For this, I used the factory pattern. (Code photo: path-to-image)

    There are also several optimization decisions, such as changing the cannon model to one with fewer polygons, baking shadows, and using the object pooling technique based on flyweight.

    Sector 7-G

    Sector 7-G is a video game that emerged from a game jam with the theme of "the calm before the storm." It involves taking on the role of a technician at a nuclear station and trying to prevent the reactor from exploding, even though the ending is inevitable (end screen note: this was my idea). It was developed in 3 days as the final project for the game art design course, in which I served as a tutor for composition and music production. However, upon learning that I could participate, I joined the group and programmed the button mini-game, as well as composed a small jazz piece on the piano. Additionally, I do not appear in the credits screen, but I am mentioned on the page.

    As is often the case with such projects and due to the participants' inexperience, the game didn't come out polished. The button mini-game lacks proper feedback. Later on, I took the project and polished it myself, but my version doesn't include the music from the other team members. Nevertheless, it is more enjoyable, and I also improved my jazz piece.
